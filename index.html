Built on Industry Standards
"Grounded in the standards the industry already follows. Organized into the system it has always needed."

The Excavation Academy curriculum is grounded in established industry standards — WorkSafe BC OHS Section 20, Alberta OHS Code Part 32, OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart P, and the Training Within Industry instructional methodology.

The knowledge comes from the industry. The system comes from T.E.A. Developed alongside leading construction professionals, regulatory bodies, and field experts across North America — organized into one accessible platform for the first time.

Civil excavation has never had a structured training standard. Every worker learned differently. Every company trained differently. Every crew figured it out the hard way. T.E.A. changes that.

The best safety program is a trained crew.

The Mission
Civil excavation built the modern world. It deserves a training system that reflects that. T.E.A. exists to create the first universal training standard for this industry — accessible to every worker, on every site, across North America.
The Format
Fully illustrated modules. Video explainers. Grade 5 reading level. Built for the worker on a phone at lunch — not in a classroom. 65 to 80% of construction workers are visual learners. T.E.A. is built around that reality.
The Standard
Grounded in WorkSafe BC OHS Section 20, Alberta OHS Code Part 32, OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart P, and Training Within Industry methodology — developed alongside leading industry professionals and regulatory frameworks across North America.
The Certification Path

Four Tiers.
One Career.

Start as a ground crew worker. Finish as someone who can build and run a civil operation. Each tier takes 6 to 8 weeks of consistent study. Each one earns a credential that travels with you.
