Frequently Asked

Common
Questions.

Do I need any experience to start?
No. Tier 1 is built for someone with zero excavation experience. Modules start with the absolute basics — utility colours, soil types, basic safety — and build from there. If you have been in the field for years, you will move faster but still pick up things nobody ever taught you.
How long does each tier take?
6 to 8 weeks per tier with consistent study. Modules are 3 to 5 minutes each. You can complete one over a coffee break or two over a lunch. Full Tier 1 — 43 modules — takes most members about 6 weeks at a comfortable pace.
Will my employer recognize the certification?
T.E.A. credentials are issued for personal training records. The curriculum is grounded in WorkSafe BC OHS Section 20, Alberta OHS Code Part 32, OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart P, and Training Within Industry methodology — the same standards regulatory bodies and major contractors already follow. If your employer requires specific union or government certifications, T.E.A. complements those rather than replaces them.
Does it work outside Canada?
Yes. The platform is built for North America wide use. Curriculum aligns with standards across Canadian provinces and US states. The physics of civil excavation does not change at any border. The principles taught — soil behaviour, utility safety, pipe installation, compaction, grade control — apply on every site.
What happens when I finish a tier?
When you complete all modules in your tier — message Cody directly inside the platform. We track your progress together and issue your T.E.A. certification credential for your records.
Is this only for civil excavation?
The core focus is civil excavation and underground construction. But many of the principles — soil science, compaction, grade control, equipment safety, business operations — apply to any heavy civil trade. Landscapers expanding into utilities, foundation contractors, road builders, and general construction professionals all benefit from the curriculum.
